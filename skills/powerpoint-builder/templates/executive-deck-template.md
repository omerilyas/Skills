# Executive Deck Template

A reusable skeleton for an executive briefing. ~10-12 slides. Pyramid Principle structure. Designed for a 15-25 minute slot with senior leadership.

Coaching notes are inline in `[BRACKETS]`. Replace placeholders, then cut anything that doesn't serve the storyline.

---

## Slide 1 — The Recommendation

**Title**: `[ONE SENTENCE — STATE THE RECOMMENDATION DIRECTLY. UNDER ~14 WORDS.]`

**Key message**: Lead with the answer. They know the ask in slide 1.

**Supporting points**:
- `[The single most compelling number — cost, time, risk, or ROI.]`
- `[The forcing function — why now, not later.]`
- `[The plan in 6 words.]`

**Visual**: `[ONE NUMBER, LARGE, CENTERED. NO BULLETS UNTIL ASKED.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[60-90 seconds. State the ask, the headline number, and what you're going to walk through. Do not explain why yet.]`

---

## Slide 2 — The Problem in One Picture

**Title**: `[NAME THE PROBLEM IN PLAIN LANGUAGE. NOT "CHALLENGES" OR "OPPORTUNITIES".]`

**Key message**: Make the problem visceral and specific. One picture is worth three slides.

**Supporting points**:
- `[The current state, quantified.]`
- `[Who is impacted and how.]`
- `[Why the status quo is unsustainable.]`

**Visual**: `[ONE DIAGRAM OR CHART THAT MAKES THE PROBLEM OBVIOUS. AVOID STOCK IMAGERY.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~90 seconds. Walk them through the picture. Don't read the bullets.]`

---

## Slide 3 — Why Now

**Title**: `[STATE THE FORCING FUNCTION. UNDER 12 WORDS.]`

**Key message**: There is a deadline, a contract renewal, a market shift, a regulatory change, or a tipping point. Name it.

**Supporting points**:
- `[The specific event or date that creates urgency.]`
- `[The cost of waiting one more cycle.]`
- `[Why this is the right window, not just any window.]`

**Visual**: `[A TIMELINE WITH THE DEADLINE MARKED.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~60 seconds. The "why now" is the second-most-important slide after slide 1. If you can't answer "why now", you don't have a recommendation, you have a wish list.]`

---

## Slide 4 — The Plan

**Title**: `[THE PLAN IN ONE LINE. PEOPLE x TIME x SHAPE.]`

**Key message**: This is a known shape of work, not a research project.

**Supporting points**:
- `[Phase 1: who, how long, deliverable.]`
- `[Phase 2: who, how long, deliverable.]`
- `[Phase 3: who, how long, deliverable.]`

**Visual**: `[GANTT-STYLE BAR OR PHASE DIAGRAM. SHOW THE SHAPE OF THE WORK, NOT JIRA TICKETS.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~90 seconds. Names of phases matter. Avoid "discovery" if you mean "we don't know yet". Be honest.]`

---

## Slide 5 — The Payback

**Title**: `[ROI IN ONE LINE — INVESTMENT, RETURN, BREAKEVEN.]`

**Key message**: The numbers are friendly. State them plainly.

**Supporting points**:
- `[Total investment, loaded.]`
- `[Annual return.]`
- `[Breakeven point in months.]`

**Visual**: `[CUMULATIVE SAVINGS / ROI CHART WITH BREAKEVEN MARKED.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~60 seconds. If your payback is over 24 months, lead with strategic value instead of ROI.]`

---

## Slide 6 — Risks and Mitigations

**Title**: `[NAME THE BIGGEST RISK AND HOW YOU DE-RISK IT. UNDER ~14 WORDS.]`

**Key message**: Name the real risk. Don't hide behind "execution risk."

**Supporting points**:
- `[Risk 1: specific, with the mitigation.]`
- `[Risk 2: specific, with the mitigation.]`
- `[A kill-switch or exit ramp if the early phase fails.]`

**Visual**: `[A SIMPLE RISK MATRIX OR A SINGLE-RISK CALLOUT WITH THE MITIGATION HIGHLIGHTED.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~90 seconds. Acknowledge the risk before they ask. Always have an exit-ramp story for senior leaders — they'll ask "what if it doesn't work in the first 30 days?"]`

---

## Slide 7 — The Alternative (Optional)

**Title**: `[WHY YOU REJECTED THE OBVIOUS ALTERNATIVE.]`

**Key message**: Pre-empt the "why not just X?" question.

**Supporting points**:
- `[The alternative they would naturally ask about.]`
- `[Why it's worse on the dimensions that matter.]`
- `[What it would cost or take to do that instead.]`

**Visual**: `[TWO-COLUMN COMPARE CARD.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~60 seconds. Drop this slide if no obvious alternative exists. But usually one does, and skipping it makes you look like you didn't consider it.]`

---

## Slide 8 — Proof / Track Record (Optional)

**Title**: `[A PRECEDENT OR TRACK RECORD THAT MAKES THIS BELIEVABLE.]`

**Key message**: Show that you've done something like this before.

**Supporting points**:
- `[Past project / team / outcome that resembles this one.]`
- `[Specific metric or result.]`
- `[Person they can verify with, if they want.]`

**Visual**: `[A SIMPLE BEFORE/AFTER OR A SHORT QUOTE WITH ATTRIBUTION.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~60 seconds. Drop this slide if you don't have a real precedent. Don't fabricate.]`

---

## Slide 9 — The Ask

**Title**: `[THE ASK, WITH THE DECISION DATE. UNDER 14 WORDS.]`

**Key message**: Specific, time-bound, with a fallback.

**Supporting points**:
- `[The primary ask in one sentence.]`
- `[The decision deadline.]`
- `[The fallback ask if the primary is too big.]`

**Visual**: `[ONE-PAGE SUMMARY CARD. ASK + DEADLINE + FALLBACK. NOTHING ELSE.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~60 seconds. End on the ask. Do not have an "any questions?" slide — invite questions verbally.]`

---

## Optional appendix

Use only if asked. Examples of slides that belong here, not in the main flow:

- Detailed financial model
- Org chart of who is doing what
- Comparison table of all considered alternatives
- Detailed risk register
- Implementation Gantt at JIRA-ticket granularity
- Vendor / tooling evaluation matrix

`[NEVER PUT APPENDIX SLIDES IN THE MAIN DECK FLOW. KEEP THEM AT THE END.]`

---

## Pacing guide

| Section                         | Slides | Time    |
| ------------------------------- | ------ | ------- |
| Recommendation + problem        | 1-2    | 3-4 min |
| Why now                         | 3      | 1 min   |
| Plan + payback                  | 4-5    | 3 min   |
| Risk + alternative + proof      | 6-8    | 3-4 min |
| The ask                         | 9      | 1 min   |
| Q&A                             | -      | 5-10 min|

Total: 15-25 minutes including Q&A.

## Common cuts

If you're over time, cut in this order:
1. Slide 8 (proof) — only if you have an internal track record they trust.
2. Slide 7 (alternative) — only if no obvious alternative exists.
3. Compress slides 4-5 into one slide (plan + payback together).

If you're under 15 minutes total, you probably have too few slides, not too many. Don't add filler. Lead with slide 1, end at slide 9, take more questions.
