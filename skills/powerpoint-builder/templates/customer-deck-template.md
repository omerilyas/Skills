# Customer Deck Template

A reusable skeleton for a customer pitch. ~9-11 slides, with optional demo bookends. Problem -> Solution -> Outcome structure. Designed for a 30-60 minute customer slot.

Coaching notes inline in `[BRACKETS]`. Replace placeholders, then cut.

---

## Slide 1 — Open With Their World, Not Yours

**Title**: `[STATE THEIR PAIN IN THEIR LANGUAGE. UNDER 14 WORDS.]`

**Key message**: This is about them. Don't open with your logo, your tagline, or "today we'll discuss."

**Supporting points**:
- `[Their specific pain, ideally a number they shared with you.]`
- `[The cost of that pain, in their currency — money, time, risk, customer trust.]`
- `[A reference to a specific event they brought up — outage, audit, deadline.]`

**Visual**: `[A QUOTE THEY SAID, OR A NUMBER THEY GAVE YOU. NOT YOUR LOGO.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~60 seconds. Tell them what you heard them say. They should feel understood before you've sold anything.]`

---

## Slide 2 — Their Pain in One Picture

**Title**: `[THE PROBLEM IN ONE LINE. NO PRODUCT NAMES YET.]`

**Key message**: Make the pain visceral. They already know it — but framing it sharply earns trust.

**Supporting points**:
- `[The mechanism that creates the pain.]`
- `[Who is impacted, by name or by role.]`
- `[Why their current approach hits a ceiling.]`

**Visual**: `[A DIAGRAM OF THEIR CURRENT STATE — THEIR STACK, THEIR PROCESS, THEIR ORG. NOT YOURS.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~90 seconds. Show that you understand the shape of their problem, not a generic version of it.]`

---

## Slide 3 — Why This Gets Worse, Not Better

**Title**: `[THE TREND OR FORCING FUNCTION THAT MAKES THIS URGENT.]`

**Key message**: The status quo is not stable. Doing nothing is the most expensive option.

**Supporting points**:
- `[A trend in their stack, market, or org that compounds the problem.]`
- `[A deadline, audit, renewal, or business event that forces a decision.]`
- `[What "wait and see" actually costs.]`

**Visual**: `[A TIMELINE OR TREND LINE SHOWING THE PROBLEM GETTING WORSE.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~60 seconds. This slide moves them from "interesting" to "we need to act".]`

---

## Slide 4 — How We Are Different

**Title**: `[THE ARCHITECTURAL OR APPROACH SHIFT IN ONE LINE. NO BUZZWORDS.]`

**Key message**: One claim, stated plainly. This is where you transition from their world to your solution.

**Supporting points**:
- `[Capability 1 — stated as an outcome, not a feature.]`
- `[Capability 2 — stated as an outcome, not a feature.]`
- `[Capability 3 — stated as an outcome, not a feature.]`

**Visual**: `[A SIMPLE DIAGRAM OF YOUR APPROACH. NO LOGOS. NO MARKETECTURE.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~90 seconds. State the differentiator once, plainly. Avoid "leveraging", "synergy", "unlock", "best-in-class". They will tune out.]`

---

## Slide 5 — Demo Bookend (Before)

**Title**: `[WHAT THEY ARE ABOUT TO SEE. UNDER 14 WORDS.]`

**Key message**: Frame the demo. Tell them what to look for. Connect it to their pain.

**Supporting points**:
- `[The scenario you're about to show.]`
- `[Three specific things to watch for.]`
- `[Why this scenario maps to their world.]`

**Visual**: `[A "WHAT TO WATCH FOR" CARD. THREE BULLETS.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~60 seconds. Then transition to the demo.]`

`[OPTIONAL — DROP THIS SLIDE IF NO DEMO. KEEP IF DEMO IS THE CENTERPIECE.]`

## Slide 6 — Demo Recap

**Title**: `[WHAT THEY JUST SAW, IN THEIR NUMBERS.]`

**Key message**: Connect what they saw to the pain you opened with.

**Supporting points**:
- `[The metric or moment that mattered most in the demo.]`
- `[How that maps to their pain from slide 1.]`
- `[A side-by-side: their current state vs. what's possible.]`

**Visual**: `[BEFORE/AFTER COMPARISON. THEIR CURRENT MTTR / COST / PROCESS, NEXT TO THE DEMO RESULT.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~60 seconds. Don't recap *every* feature in the demo. Recap the ONE thing that maps to slide 1.]`

`[OPTIONAL — DROP IF NO DEMO.]`

---

## Slide 7 — Proof Point

**Title**: `[A SIMILAR-SHAPE CUSTOMER OUTCOME. ONE LINE.]`

**Key message**: We have done this for someone like you. Specific, with a number.

**Supporting points**:
- `[Reference customer (named or under NDA — be honest about which).]`
- `[The specific outcome with the metric.]`
- `[Why their stack / scale / industry resembles this customer's.]`

**Visual**: `[A BEFORE/AFTER METRIC CHART OR A SHORT QUOTE WITH ATTRIBUTION.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~60 seconds. If you don't have a same-scale, same-industry reference, say so. Don't stretch a reference further than it goes.]`

---

## Slide 8 — Trust / Differentiation

**Title**: `[THE THING THAT MAKES YOU CREDIBLE BEYOND THE FEATURE LIST.]`

**Key message**: Why us, beyond the product. Optional but high-impact.

**Supporting points**:
- `[A team / track record / partnership / certification that establishes credibility.]`
- `[A specific operational practice (e.g. "we run this for [scale])".]`
- `[An honest constraint or "what we don't do" — earns trust.]`

**Visual**: `[A CREDIBILITY CARD — TEAM, CUSTOMERS, OR A SPECIFIC OPERATIONAL FACT.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~45 seconds. Optional. Drop if it would feel salesy in this room.]`

---

## Slide 9 — The Ask

**Title**: `[THE NEXT STEP, SPECIFIC AND SMALL.]`

**Key message**: Make the next step easy. Time-boxed and low-friction.

**Supporting points**:
- `[Primary ask: e.g. 30-day PoC scoped to [their highest-pain area]].`
- `[Their definition of success — they set the bar.]`
- `[What you commit to: people, deliverables, communication cadence.]`

**Visual**: `[A POC SCOPE CARD: SCOPE, DURATION, EXIT CRITERIA.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~60 seconds. The specific scope is more persuasive than a general "let's work together".]`

---

## Slide 10 — Two Paths Forward

**Title**: `[TWO CLEAR NEXT STEPS — PICK ONE.]`

**Key message**: Always offer a fallback. SEs who walk in with one ask walk out with no ask.

**Supporting points**:
- `[Path A: the bigger commitment — PoC, contract, deeper engagement.]`
- `[Path B: the smaller commitment — workshop, architecture review, reference call.]`
- `[Either way: you own the next move and the timeline.]`

**Visual**: `[TWO-PATH FORK DIAGRAM.]`

**Speaker notes**: `[~45 seconds. End with the explicit choice. "Which one fits better?"]`

## Pacing guide

| Section                              | Slides | Time     |
| ------------------------------------ | ------ | -------- |
| Open with their world                | 1-2    | 4-5 min  |
| Why now                              | 3      | 2 min    |
| How we're different                  | 4      | 3 min    |
| Demo bookend + demo + recap          | 5,6    | 12-15 min|
| Proof + trust                        | 7-8    | 3-4 min  |
| Ask + next steps                     | 9-10   | 3 min    |
| Q&A                                  | -      | 10 min   |

Total: 35-45 minutes. Adjust by trimming demo time or cutting slides 7 / 8.

## Common cuts

In order:
1. Slide 8 (trust) — drop if light on credibility material; the demo + proof slide can carry it.
2. Slide 7 (proof) — only if you have a strong demo and a credible spoken track record.
3. Slide 3 (why now) — only if the customer already volunteered the urgency in discovery.

Slides 1, 2, 4, 9, 10 are non-negotiable.

## Banned phrases

`[REPLACE THESE EVERY TIME YOU SEE THEM IN A DRAFT:]`

- "Best-in-class"
- "World-class"
- "Leverage"
- "Synergy"
- "Unlock value"
- "End-to-end solution"
- "Holistic approach"
- "Seamless integration"

Replace with concrete language. If you can't, the claim probably wasn't real.
