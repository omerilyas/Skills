# Example: Market Snapshot Output

This is an illustrative example of how the Research Agent structures a market / business synthesis. The topic, vendors, and figures are placeholders chosen to show the **shape** of a good response — not real data.

> Topic (illustrative): "Current state of the enterprise [category-X] market and where pricing is heading."

---

# Executive Summary

The enterprise [category-X] market is consolidating around a small number of platform vendors while a long tail of specialists competes on niche workloads. List prices are stable, but effective prices are falling through bundling and committed-use discounts. Buyers should expect another 12–18 months of aggressive packaging before list prices move materially.

# Key Findings

- Three to five platform vendors capture the majority of net-new enterprise spend.
- Specialist vendors retain pricing power in regulated or latency-sensitive niches.
- Effective discounts of 20–40% off list are increasingly common in multi-year deals.
- Open-source alternatives are pulling smaller deals down-market but rarely displace platform vendors at the enterprise tier.

# Evidence

- Public pricing pages and published reference architectures from the major vendors.
- Earnings-call commentary on net-revenue retention and discounting trends.
- Independent analyst reports (paid) and a small set of practitioner surveys.

# Conflicting Views

- Vendor-sponsored research tends to overstate consolidation; independent analyst data shows a slower pace.
- Practitioners disagree on whether open-source alternatives are "good enough" — answers correlate with team size and tolerance for self-hosting.

# Risks / Caveats

- Survey samples skew toward respondents already using a paid product.
- Pricing trends can reverse quickly if a major vendor changes packaging.
- Regulatory shifts in data residency could fragment the market by geography.

# Confidence Level

**Medium.** Directional trends are well supported across independent sources; precise share and discount figures depend on private data the example does not have.

# Sources Consulted

- Vendor pricing pages and product documentation.
- Vendor earnings calls and investor materials.
- Independent industry analyst reports.
- Practitioner surveys with disclosed methodology.

> Reminder: this is a format example. Real outputs must cite real sources, not source *types*.
